Fun Facts About Cities

New York

  • The first pizzeria in the United States opened in New York City in 1895.
  • New York City is made up of five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island.
  • Central Park is larger than the principality of Monaco.

London

  • London has over 170 museums, with the British Museum being one of the most famous.
  • There are more than 300 languages spoken in London.
  • Big Ben is not the name of the clock tower, but the bell inside it.

Paris

  • The Eiffel Tower was initially intended to be dismantled after 20 years.
  • Paris has the second highest number of Michelin-starred restaurants in the world.
  • The Louvre Museum in Paris is the largest art museum in the world.

Rome

  • Rome's Colosseum is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
  • Vatican City, the smallest country in the world, is located within Rome.
  • Rome has more fountains than any other city in the world.

Bangkok

  • Bangkok's real name is one of the longest place names in the world.
  • Bangkok is home to more than 400 temples.
  • The city is known for having some of the world's largest street markets.

Amman

  • Amman is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world.
  • The Citadel in Amman is home to the ruins of a Hercules temple.
  • Amman is known for its ancient Roman theater, which is still in use today.
